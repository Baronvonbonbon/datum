## Minimum viable CPM — economics by batching cadence

When users batch their own impressions and settle directly (no commercial relay
taking a margin), the binding economic constraint is the user's own settlement-tx
gas cost. This section derives the minimum CPM (DOT per 1,000 impressions) at
which a user breaks even on a self-settle workflow at various batching cadences.

**Revenue split assumption:** publisher 50% / user 37.5% / protocol 12.5%
(per the BM-ECO benchmark suite). Users earn 37.5% of CPM × imps_settled.

**Gas model (linear fit from measurements):**
- 1-claim batch: 539,688 gas
- 5-claim batch: 660,529 gas
- 10-claim batch: 979,759 gas
- Fitted: `gas(n) = 509,478 + 30,210 × n`

Modelling reward-per-impression in **DOT-equivalent** (i.e., reward × DATUM/DOT spot price):

## Interpretation

### Per-relay annual cost recap (5 gwei)

| Tier | DOT/yr | Per-claim gas |
|---|---:|---:|
| Hobby (1 batch/hr × 1 claim) | 23.64 | 539,688 (no amortisation) |
| Standard (1 batch/hr × 5 claims) | 28.93 | 132,106 |
| Heavy (10 batches/hr × 10 claims) | 429.13 | 97,976 |
| Hyper (1 batch/min × 10 claims) | 2574.81 | 97,976 |

Linear-fit marginal cost: `gas(n_claims) ≈ 509,478 + 30,210 × n`.
Bigger batches get dramatically cheaper per claim — at 10 claims/batch, per-claim
gas drops from 539,688 to 97,976.
Relay operators should batch as aggressively as latency tolerance allows.

### Headline finding

With users self-settling and a monthly batching cadence at conservative Hub pricing,
the minimum CPM for the user side to be net-positive on gas fees is
**0.031 DOT per 1,000 impressions**. At $5/DOT that's ~$0.15 CPM
— over an order of magnitude below traditional web2 CPMs ($1–5).

### Practical implications

- **The user side dominates.** Publishers and advertisers break even at CPMs
  100–300× lower than what users need.
- **Higher-traffic users dominate proportionally less.** A user with 36k imps/yr
  breaks even at CPM ≈ 0.003 DOT — 10× cheaper than the 3,650-imp baseline.
- **Cadence is the lever, not contract optimisation.** Switching users from daily
  to monthly batching drops the break-even CPM 24×. Yearly batching drops it 80×.
- **At-scale viability is comfortable.** Real-world programmatic CPMs ($0.50–$5)
  sit 15–300× above the break-even floor at conservative pricing.
- **Worst-case (50 gwei × daily batching)** still bottoms out at CPM ≈ 7.3 DOT
  = ~$36/CPM — above traditional rates, but only in pathological combinations.
- The protocol's incentive design should encourage users to batch monthly+ via
  UI defaults and/or DATUM token rewards for delayed settlement.
