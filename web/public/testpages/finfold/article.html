Rates, unpacked: what the curve is quietly saying

A plain-English read on an inverted yield curve that refuses to behave.

The curve has been a reliable recession bell for decades, which is exactly why this cycle has economists hedging. The signal is loud; the timing is not.

Strip out the noise and the message is about expectations: markets think short rates come down, eventually. The fight is over 'when'.

For ordinary savers the takeaway is unglamorous — ladder your maturities and stop trying to time the top.
